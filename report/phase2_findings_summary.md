# Phase 2 Findings Summary

## Dataset inspected
- Source: UN Comtrade raw CSV uploaded by user
- Reporter: Thailand
- Flow: Import
- Commodity: HS8542 Electronic integrated circuits
- Period: 2014–2024
- Raw records: 1,178

## Main result
Thailand's HS8542 integrated circuit imports increased from **$9.68B in 2014** to **$24.43B in 2024**, a total increase of **152.3%**.

Imports from **Other Asia, nes** — used in this project as a **Taiwan/Chinese Taipei proxy with limitations** — increased from **$2.21B in 2014** to **$10.65B in 2024**, a total increase of **382.6%**.

The Taiwan proxy dependency index increased from **22.8% in 2014** to **43.6% in 2024**.

## Peak dependency
The highest dependency level in the dataset is **43.6% in 2024**.

## Suggested report wording
> Thailand's HS8542 import data shows a rising concentration of integrated circuit imports from `Other Asia, nes`, which is used in this study as a Taiwan/Chinese Taipei proxy due to partner classification limitations. The proxy share increased from 22.8% in 2014 to 43.6% in 2024, suggesting that Thailand's exposure to Taiwan-related semiconductor supply chain disruption has increased over the past decade.

## Limitation wording
> Because `Other Asia, nes` may include minor flows from other unspecified Asian economies, this figure should be interpreted as an estimate of Taiwan-related exposure rather than an exact official Taiwan import share.
